# DropsAtLubis  
### Numerical Modeling of Droplet Behavior in Lubrication and Fluid Systems

This repository contains a computational and physical modeling framework dedicated to the study of **droplet dynamics in lubrication environments**.  
The project focuses on the interaction between fluid mechanics, surface physics, and numerical simulation methods.

The objective is to analyze how droplets behave under different physical conditions relevant to:

- lubrication systems
- fluid transport
- thermal-fluid processes
- surface interaction phenomena
- engineering fluid mechanics

---

## Scientific Context

Droplet dynamics in lubricated systems is a fundamental topic in:

- fluid mechanics
- tribology
- lubrication engineering
- thermal-fluid systems
- industrial fluid transport
- mechanical system efficiency

Understanding droplet behavior allows engineers to:

- optimize lubrication performance
- reduce friction and wear
- improve energy efficiency
- predict fluid distribution
- design reliable mechanical systems

This repository contributes to the numerical and physical analysis of droplet motion and interaction in lubricated environments.

---

## Objectives

The main goals of this project are:

- model droplet motion in lubrication systems
- simulate fluid behavior under physical constraints
- analyze flow regimes and surface interaction
- support engineering analysis through numerical simulation
- develop reproducible computational workflows
